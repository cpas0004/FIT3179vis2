Short and Long-Term Health Impacts

During the fires, it is estimated that over 75% of Australiaâ€™s population was subject to prolonged smoke inhalation. On Jan 14th 2020, the density of particulates in the air peaked at a level 14 times higher than ever before. The smoke lead to over 3000 hospital admissions for cardiovascular and respiratory problems and 1300 ER visits for asthma. The effects can be seen in the numbers of prescription inhaler medications dispensed peaking throughout the fires, which is visualised on the left. According to the Menzies Institute, bushfire smoke was the cause of 400 deaths across the country in 2019-2020.
